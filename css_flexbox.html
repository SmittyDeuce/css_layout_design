The Largest Tornadoes in History

Tri-State Tornado (1925)

The Tri-State Tornado remains one of the most catastrophic tornadoes in U.S. history. On March 18, 1925, this tornado struck Missouri, Illinois, and Indiana, causing widespread destruction and claiming the lives of over 600 people. Its path of devastation stretched over 200 miles.

El Reno Tornado (2013)

The El Reno Tornado, which occurred on May 31, 2013, is the widest tornado ever recorded, measuring 2.6 miles across. It hit near El Reno, Oklahoma, and brought extreme wind speeds, contributing to its status as one of the most powerful tornadoes in recent history.

Joplin Tornado (2011)

On May 22, 2011, the Joplin Tornado struck Joplin, Missouri, with devastating force. This EF-5 tornado caused significant damage, resulting in 155 fatalitiesand extensive destruction across the city, making it one of the deadliest tornadoes in recent decades.

Dallas Tornado (1957)

The Dallas Tornado of April 2, 1957, swept through Dallas, Texas, with high intensity, causing considerable damage and loss of life. It remains one of the most severe tornadoes to impact a major U.S. city.

Goliad Tornado (1902)

The Goliad Tornado hit Goliad, Texas, on August 27, 1902. Though less documented, this tornado is known for its significant impact and the destruction it caused in the area.

Snyder Tornado (1970)

The Snyder Tornado, occurring on May 26, 1970, affected Snyder, Texas. It was a powerful tornado that resulted in notable damage and is remembered for its strength and the devastation it caused.

Lubbock Tornado (1970)

The Lubbock Tornado, which struck on May 11, 1970, was a major tornado that impacted Lubbock, Texas. It was part of a severe weather outbreak that led to substantial destruction in the area.

Woodward Tornado (1947)

On April 9, 1947, the Woodward Tornado impacted Woodward, Oklahoma. This tornado is noted for its intensity and the severe damage it inflicted on the region.

Tuscaloosa-Birmingham Tornado (2011)

The Tuscaloosa-Birmingham Tornado of April 27, 2011, was a massive tornado that affected Alabama, particularly Tuscaloosa and Birmingham. It was one of the most significant tornadoes of the 2011 Super Outbreak.

St. Louis Tornado (1927)

The St. Louis Tornado struck St. Louis, Missouri, on March 29, 1927. It caused severe damage and remains one of the significant tornadoes in the early 20th century.
